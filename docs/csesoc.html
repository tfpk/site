Thomas Kunc (Running for 2020 CSESoc Treasurer and Grievance Officer)

Bio

My name is Tom. I'm a second year Computer Science student at UNSW. I tutor and admin COMP1511, and help out with other first year courses. I don't want to spam you, so I've writen a few paragraphs about why I'm running for Treasurer and Grievance Officer

I'm passionate about making CSESoc useful for everyone, and, if elected, would focus on making events and programs that were useful for every student (undergrad and postgrad; and no matter where they're from).

Grievence officer is a new position, so I want to figure out what role it can play in the community - if elected, I'll try to be as consultative as possible with every member of CSESoc about what they want. I envision a committee of people who can talk to new members, and are available to the rest of the society as consultants for making events more accessible and inclusive - for instance, a group of post-graduate students we can ask about running events for postgraduates. I would also ensure that First Year Camp is run in an accessible way for everyone - whether they party hard, or hardly party.

As Treasurer, my main intent would be to create tools to make the society's finances easily inspected and received. I would like to explore setting up software to automate a large part of the Treasurer's role away, leading to faster reimbursements and more clarity about the society's finances.

Experience

As CSESoc Projects director, I helped manage a team of 50 people and 10 projects. This involved organising interviews, and events. We also had to manage a budget, and check in with our teams to make sure they were being effective.

As COMP1511 Course Admin I've been responsible for over 1200 students, managing my time and resources to ensure timely marking. As a tutor, I've taught around 200 students.

The other society I participate in is the UNSW debating society. They already have an eqivalent of the grievance officer (called an Equity officer) and I would intend to take some of their ideas and apply them here.
